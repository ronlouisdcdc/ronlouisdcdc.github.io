Introduction 

     EJK or extrajudicial killings has been one of the most controversial and widely topical issues of the Philippines. Associated with the now current president, President Rodrigo Duterte, EJK had equated to the equally controversial war on drugs, which seemingly targeted people who are against the government. However, the citizens, who differ in age, races and class, still have different opinions and perceptions regarding this issue. For example, if EJK really exists in our country or not, and if it is appropriate in order to conduct the campaign against drugs.

     With the influence of social media and the internet, the younger generation are now fully aware of the relevant affairs circling the country, in which a lot have expressed their strong opinions and beliefs regarding the particular topic. With this, a survey was conducted at the 19th of November 2018 to the Criminology students of Bulacan State University, aiming to know the socio-demographics of the students in the College of Criminal Justice Education major in Criminology. 100 questionnaires were given to 100 respondents, wherein 50 are male and 50 are female. The questions or statements on the questionnaire were to be ranked by scale, where 5 is strongly agree and 1 is strongly disagree.

     The objective of this survey is to know how the future lawmen perceive extrajudicial killings in the Philippines.

Analysis
     Here are some simple tabular representations of the survey’s outcome with a brief explanation:

     Table 1: I support the campaign against illegal drugs of the administration of the president.

     With the data illustrated in Table 1, it is evident that majority of the respondents is truly in favor of our current president’s campaign, with the majority, a total of 60%, answering strongly agree to the statement.

     Table 2: I believe that extrajudicial killings exists because of the campaign against illegal drugs.

     Table 2 shows the respondents’ affirmativeness to the case that extrajudicial killings sprouted due to president Duterte’s campaign against illegal drugs, where 21% strongly agreed and 37% agreed. 

     Table 3: I think extrajudicial killings are justified through this year’s crime rate drop in the Philippines.

     60% of the respondents both agreed and strongly agreed that extrajudicial killings are vindicated due to the Philippines’ crime rate drop this year.
     Table 4: With the current state of our country, we must focus on salvaging it even to the point of using extreme measures.

     Majority of the respondents, 46%, has a neutral opinion regarding the country’s response to its deteriorating state, however, 37% of the respondents agreed and strongly agreed to use extreme measures.

     Table 5: Any Filipino who uses illegal drugs should die without legal due process.

     Half of the one hundred respondents believe that even illegal drug users should not die without the appropriate and legal due process.

     Table 6: I am satisfied with the current administration’s campaign against illegal drugs.

     With the data illustrated in Table 6, it is evident that majority of the respondents are satisfied with the infamous campaign against illegal drugs.

    Table 7: I believe that extrajudicial killing is simply our country’s combat against drugs and crimes.

     According to the results, 11% of the respondents strongly agreed and 39% agreed that extrajudicial killings have been deemed as the country’s solution to drugs and crimes.

     Table 8: The decrease of issues involving illegal drugs after the president’s campaign is a sign of economic advancement.

     With the data shown in Table 8, it is evident that majority, 60%, of the respondents believe that the decrease of drug-related issues in the Philippines equates to a rising economy.

     Table 9: I think extrajudicial killing is the cause of other countries’ bad perception of the Philippines.

     61% of the respondents both agreed and strongly agreed that extrajudicial killings changed the international community’s perception of Philippines negatively.

     Table 10: Nobody deserves to experience the cruelty of extrajudicial killings.

     Based from the tabulated data, it is apparent that the respondents believe that extrajudicial killings should not be experienced by anyone.

     Table 11: I would say that the victims of extrajudicial killings are all criminals.
     According to the results, 41% of the respondents believes that the victims of extra judicial killings are not only limited to criminals, but can also involve other individuals as well.

     Table 12: If the country’s citizens would simply follow its strict rules and regulations, they would not worry about extrajudicial killing.
     Most of the respondents, roughly 69%, believe that extrajudicial killings would not happen to anyone if he/she was to simply follow the country’s rules and regulations.

     Table 13: I feel safer when President Duterte’s administration started.
     Table 13 shows the respondents’ sense of security with the current administration where 52% of them responded both agree and strongly agree.

     Table 14: Extrajudicial killings and forced disappearances in the Philippines are illegal, unlawful and felonious executions.
     54% of the respondents are in agreement with the statement which antagonizes extrajudicial killings in the Philippines.

     Table 15: Extrajudicial killings are most commonly referred as “salvaging” in the Philippines.
     Majority of the respondents, 49% in total, believe that EJK is just a way to rescue and recover the glory of our country.

     Table 16: Seemingly, extrajudicial killing is the quick answer to those who committed heinous crimes and those who are involved with drugs.
     With the data illustrated in Table 16, 44% of the respondents believes that extrajudicial killing is just fitting due to its “effectiveness” in disposing criminals in the country.

     Table 17: I believe that the police are not abusing their powers against the general public, rather, just following their received commands.
     Table 17 shows that the respondents defend the police force by agreeing to the presented statement, where 19% answers strongly agree and 42% answers agree.

     Table 18: I think that extrajudicial killings are only targeting small time drug users and pushers, and are leaving the real drug lords in protection of the government.
     Based from the gathered data, 57% of the respondents agrees and strongly agrees with the observation that the victims of extrajudicial killings are the minor drug pusher, but not the major drug lords.

     Table 19: Dozens of similar killings have taken place almost daily in the Philippines, but with drugs and crime so deep-rooted, there is barely any public outrage nor awareness.
     Most of the respondents, 14% strongly agrees and 41% agrees, thinks that reason why most of the killings remained unpublicized is due to the profound issue of drugs in the Philippines.

     Table 20: If I were to continue and pursue my current vocation as planned, I would refuse to commit any inhumane deed, especially those related to extrajudicial killings.
     72% of the respondents assures that they would never use any inhumane acts in retrieving peace and justice in the Philippines.

Conclusion
     Based on the data gathered from the Criminology students of Bulacan State University and the tabulation of result, the survey shows that: 

          • The male and female respondents have congruent idea on the given question that show
     the same notion of the respondents which may lead to more accurate data and unanimous
     perception in both sexes.

          • With all the given questions in the questionnaire, the respondents often have high
     percentage of answering neutral to a certain question displaying trace of falsity in the
     answers, resulting to inaccuracy of the data.

          • The tabulated percentage scores shows that majority of the Criminology students
     perceive EJK as an alarming issue, requiring further legal action, involving not just lawmakers
     and the higher authority, but also the greater population of ordinary citizens. 

Recommendations
     With the tabulated data and concluded result, the surveyors recommend the following for future surveyors of the said topic:

          • Future surveyors should first determine the perception and ideology of the Criminology
     students on the term “Extrajudicial killings”.

          • Future surveyors should instigate the further consequences of extrajudicial killings on an      ordinary student, particularly on the participants’ everyday lives. 

          • Future surveyors should eliminate the neutral choice to garner a more concrete answer,
     resulting to a more accurate data.

          • Since the survey was conducted on a smaller student body, we recommend that future
     surveyors conduct the survey on a larger scope to further test if the result can be
     generalized on a diversified scale.
