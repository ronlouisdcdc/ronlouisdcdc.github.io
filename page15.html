Reflection Paper

#ThinkPositive #BePositive #StayPositive

    Life is like a twisted game of tag. One minute you feel like the happiest person alive, then next minute, ‘Tag, you are just a failure of a teen trying to fit in!’. Then it hits you hard; you are it. The catch is, you are not able to tag somebody else. Once you are it, the negative emotion remains until God knows when. It is awful: being on this continuous cycle of positivity and negativity, being exhausted of shaking away the pursuer only to reach your limits and be tagged, and being hopeless about not being able to tag another person so as not to dwell on negative emotions. Fortunately, there is this annual tradition which casts off negativity and serves as a fresh new start - the New Year’s Resolution.

     As each year passes by, a book with lingering scents of newly issued paper and ink is unearthed. It would then unfold blank pages where we, the authors, are expected to write on; where we are expected to engrave every chance, every sentiment, and every story which would make our life worthwhile, or, in this case, worth reading. It is an opportunity to make up for the regrets and negativity of the past year; a new year to become a better version of one’s self, if you may.

     Personally, my 2018 conundrum was about me being negative. To my defense, it was a gloomy year! Early 2018 was filled with bittersweet memories built by a tightknit class yearning to have an extension of their shared fantasy. Inaudible screams of ‘please stay’ echoed. Life decisions were made, and the inseparable bond of classmates slowly faded. On the other hand, the latter part of my 2018 was not easy either. The transition into a new school, after studying 12 years in the old one, is no joke. The anxiety of belongingness is still a thing I am working on, since forming new bonds was alien to me. Henceforth, my new year’s resolution is positivity.

     But the question is, how do we avoid ever being ‘tagged’? It is about investments… Not the monetary kind, no. Rather, investing on opportunities which may propagate one’s holistic change and development, investing on charisma to get away with shameful situations, and most of all, investing on perception, because everything has its positive and negative side, and it is up to us which side to perceive and live by.

     Eventually, while writing, I came to a realization – that the latter investment is the main source of positivity. Yes, positivity can come from avoiding regrets or dodging shameful situations, but these terrible circumstances are superficial if you see the world in a positive lens; if one becomes optimistic. A life with constant happiness seems unattainable, but it basically just about treating the glass half-full.

     Conclusively, life is not a game of tag, but a tug-of-war between your positive and negative emotions. And, it is up to you whose side you will help win.

     To end this discourse, allow me to part lyrics from song which helped me be regain positivity during negative moments:

     “Maybe it’s not my weekend, but it’s gonna be my year
      And I'm so sick of watching while the minutes pass as I go nowhere
      And this is my reaction to everything I fear
      'Cause I've been going crazy, I don't wanna waste another minute here
