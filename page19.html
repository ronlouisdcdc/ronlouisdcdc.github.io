If I Could Change the World, I Would...
     If we live in a perfect world, we could wander at night alone without fear or hesitation... If we live in a perfect world, we could stay outside without being scorched by the sun... If we live
in a perfect world, we could be as one... But nothing exudes perfection!

     Today, the modern world is light years away from being perfect. Though it is the era of modernization and technology, ironically, our values, our unity, and our world deteriorate; as we  take a step forward for an easier life, we take steps backward from our humanity. If I could change the world, I would make innovation possible with without worsening, but improving, the current situation of the three said facets - values, unity, and the world itself.

     Changing the world must start with changing oneself. Hence I would start by improving its inhabitants, the people, by turning them into more considerate and affectionate citizens. If we 
were to recognize values, morals and ethics more, and be less crazed by the easy and modern
quality of life, we would stop striving to live easier, but actually live, without physically destroying  the world or emotionally destroying its residents. That, for me, is the highest quality of life. Doing so, it would also cause the minds of the future leaders of the world to change; to be more like Nelson Mandela or Mahatma Gandhi.

     Another cause of chaos in this world is the difference of beliefs, and bigotry towards unknown ones. Everybody is entitled to their own opinions, but everybody must also pay respect to others' convictions. If we learn how to accept and love cach other without judging him/her based on religion, color, nationality, social status, or gender, we could restore the humanity we have lost to the lust of technology. We would also avoid catastrophic wars, for I believe that the main cause of it is the conflict of ideas. Hence I would unify the world by showering all kinds of principles with love and respect, resulting to a beautiful, steady waltz of different colors, different beliefs, and different kinds of people.

     Once the cause is plucked, the symptoms must then be mended. The current physical situation of our world now is truly frightening and is clearly in need of change. Thus, if I were to 
change the world, I would make it the best world to live on, starting with the reconditioning of the  depleting ozone layer. By turning it back to its original, undestroyed form, it would generate a domino effect turning the world back to its former glory. It would end global warming and climate change by decreasing the ultraviolet rays from the sun, which would lower and stabilize the currently rising sea level.

     With the current situation of our Mother Earth right now, we cannot make the world perfect, but we can strive for it to be. With the sufficient amount of time, effort and discipline, the world we knew and loved might come back.

     Allow me to end this essay with lyrics from a song by Miehael Jackson.

     "Heal the world. Make it a better place. For you and for me and the entire human race."
